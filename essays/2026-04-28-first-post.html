One of the hardest questions to answer in life is what to do? The the reason why I think it is such a hard question to answer because it isnt a single question but rather a combination of other questions which must be answered in series. When I ask most people what should I do the responses are remarkably the same desipte the varity of different ways to achieve it. All the answers are to do what you love or are interested in. But how do you do what you love? Well the first step is to figure out what you love and that could only be done through experience. Doing what you love is so hard because figuring out what you love is so hard. Figuring out what you love is sort of like a blind person waving around their stick looking for objects. For example If you are interested in the field of medecine you can explore it but a majority of the time you are doing it blindly. Imagine life as a sort of maze with the grand prize of doing what you love in the middle and you have to figure out how to get there. Now imagine each respective field as its own turn off of the main one in which you start. The only way you can get to the middle is by running through the maze or banging against the walls. Take the medicine example, first you take a turn down the medicine hallway and start exploring. After the initial branch you pass other hallways that are subdivisions of medicine. Imagine you pass a hallway with nursing, and then with Neurology and then Dermatology and so on and so on. Then you go for there still holding true to the internal compass of following curiosity. The hard part is that the original path will not lead you to the center but may be a dead end. As a result you have to start over again or you may be so far down the road you may just be lost. A lot of people fall into the last part where they areso far down the road that they stay lost. This was trure for my Grandfather. He was interested in the field of medicing and went through the army and became a field medic. It was only after he spent 2 years as a field medic where he realized that he hated the job. But since he has spent so long achieving the goal he just sucked it up because it payed the bills. In addition when I asked him if I should puruse it he physically shook me and told me not to. In his words it is nothing like the stuff on T.V. and he just wasted so much time. Now how do I even begin to pursue something that when I achieve it I might not like it? My answer to this is that the worst case scenario is not that bad and the best case scenario is so much better. The main fear is derived from societal pressures of being rich and powerful. But a lot of people are faking which in turn makes the societal pressures fake. I have a firm belief that the majority of people are in jobs they hate which makes the majority opinion derived from people who hate their jobs. If you look at the high end of the spectrum It is a lot of entreprenuers who have a lot of money. They have a lot of money because they are good at what they do. They are good at what they do because they enjoy what they do. Furthermore a lot of people on the other end of the spectrum are people are people who arent rich because they arent good at what they do. They arent good at what they do because they dont like it. So why should you take the opinion of someone who doesnt even like what they do. Now the best case scenario concept is not that hard to grasp as there is evidence in every life. A rich person leaving their job after they made enough money to go do pottery or live alone in the mountains. The hedgefund/tech billionares are reverting to the simple life of painting or spending time with family should provide evidence that, this type of life is worth so much more than all the moeny they made. Which proves that doing what you love is dispraportionatley more rewarding than a pile of money even if that pile is 200B+. Now if the reward of doing what you love is so high, how high is the probability of this outcome happening. Well the thing is the probability increases the longer or harder you "poke life". Poking life reduces to taking action of ones own life. Poking life is liek poking a very levelheaded bear. If you poke a little bit or every so often the bear wont react. But if you poke it alot or very hard it will react but once it does there is a whole storm of things coming with them (typically positive). throughout I have not poked life very hard or banged into the walls to much. As a result I havent reaped much a reward. But I have friends who have literally poked life so much that it gives immense reawrds in hods the user stops.
