Bad Tests

April 29, 2026

Why are grades so disproportionatley more important than actually learnig the material? I think this is done because there are 2 groups of people who are achieving the same thing but do it in different ways/ have different goals. Case 1 is sort of a velvet rope reasoning. A lot of my professors know that grades are not a measurment of intelligence but rather your first test as a "problem solver". My highschool biology techer made it her mission to "hide" answers throughout the test therefore if you didnt study that specific part you could figure it out. I believe this teacher is blatantly saying if you want to be a problem solver your first test is to figure out how to game the system and get an A. Therefore incentivinsing the students to figure out a way to game her sytem in order to become a better problem solver. Case 2 are people who thoroughly believe that GPA is a correlation with intelligence and treat people accordingly. In case 1 you would view intelligence as a checking the box feature. For example, if 2 people have a GPA differnce of around .5 they are smart and their intelligence could only be measured through acomplishments. Imagine 2 math majors one has a 4.0 and another has a 3.8 but the 3.8 has published paper. If I where to ask you who is more intelligent many people would say the 3.8 canidate. I would have to disagree I believe that the 3.8 has more determination than 4.0 but they both have the same intelectual abilities. So one isnt smarter but rather more determined. But for someone in case 2 you would have the belief that their intelligence levels arent similar whether that the 4.0 is smarter than the 3.8 or the 3.8 smarter than the 4.0. People with this belife typically means their system is easier to game. For example in one of my classes the professor often told me that I was smart and smarter than other students in the class because I was an engineering major. There is a correlation between major and intelligence but it is mostly bullshit. I would argue most english majors or philosphy majors have a better conceptual understanding of life but cant solve a differential equation. Does that make me smarter than them? No of course not but yet someone still had this belief. This very same professor pulled up ChatGPT to ask it to clarify a definition for a word on the test. On the side bar there are numerous past prompts with the titles of "Exam Creation" and "Exam building help. The COLLEGE PROFESSOR just showed that they used AI to create an exam to give to students. Furethermore they didnt know what the word in their own exam meant because they didnt make it. If someone weighs GPA as a measure of intelligence then it is more attractive to try and game the sytem with low effort becasue the pay off is huge(being called smart). Therefore we can induce a new rule of thumb, whatever the professor does is par for the course. As a result if you use AI to create a worksheet for me then it is par for the course for me to use AI to complete that very assignment. These 2 cases of people both view the GPA in a different light yet they both push the student to try and game the system in order to achieve good grades. How could you expect to solve any major problem if you cant even beat out a lousy professor in order to get a 4.0. In this specific case a 4.0 is a signal of inteligence because you are figuring out how to solve the problem of getting good grades. How could you take anyone seriously who didnt have a high GPA because that means they couldnt figure out the game the system. But that is were the comparison ends. If getting good grades is the velvet rope than the other side is the "Smart club". Once your in the club you can't tell who is smarter than each other but everyone in the club is most definetly smarter than everyone outside. It is the first test but not the last as there are clubs inside the club. One of the most obvious clubs in the world is where you go to school. The "club" is publicly known as Ivy+ which are all the ivy league schools plus the top tech ones such as MIT,Stanford,Caltech etc. But wait if the only way you can get into a club is by gaming the application process does that mean that all Ivy+ schools can be gamed? Yes that is the point and there is evidence of this. The mere existance of prepschools prove my point as they can take the very same applicant and add some pretty fluff. By now everyone knows how to get into a good school and the only way is by extra curriculars. You may think that if you want to go to a good school you need good SAT/GPA but these are table stakes in applying to these "clubs". If you were to think about all the the "Tests" you had to pass in chronological given that you are going to the public highschool. It goes get a 4.0, get a 1500+ on the SAT and do extracurriculars. How you beat every test is by gaming the system. For the 4.0 all you need to do is beat bad tests. Lets define a good test as a test that cannot be gamed and accurately represents the testtakers knowlegde of the subject at that specific time. There for a bad test would be a something that does not accurately represent the testtakers knowlegde of the subject at that specific time. Using this defintion I believe most exams are bad tests. They are bad because someone with a low level of understanding can score higher than they deserve and a person with a high level of understanding can score lower than they deserve. If we are trying to beat bad tests in order to get into the club then we need to figure out a way to score higher than our level of understanding and exploit that difference to the absolute maximum. Now how do you exploit bad tests and the asnwer is memorizing riddles. If you are giving a Q&A type exam then you are simply testing declarative knowledge so as a result all you would need is wikipedia in you back pocket and you would be all set. But since you cant openly have a phone in the exam this method takes different shapesThis comes in the forms of looking at similar problems and trying to map solutions onto other problems or just figuring out what questions could be asked. A lot of my exams come down to can you memorize the riddle. There are only so many different practice problems you can throw at me before I just memorize the steps/language in order to get a good grade. I am fairly confident if you somehow were given the math 55 final exam answer key before the exam you can get a pretty good grade despite having no clue about the theory or what is going on. I actually took an exam today and I programed all the formulas into my calcualtor with the inputs being the given dimensions and was out of there in 20 mintutes. I manually gamed the system. In reality a good exam would be me standing up infront of the room and not doing calculations but explain the though process and what each lever would do when pulled. The exam was on the design of machine elements and there is a mindless formula that a computer can do with the simple 4 functions of +-*/. Despite me not knowing what was going on I am still able to maintain a 98 avergae in the class after 15 weeks of lectures. The main point of this whole thing is that most exams are bad and the benefit you get from beating them is not representative of the work achieved. If you beat an exam depending on the person evaluating you, you either became a better problem solver or are regarded as smart. In reality the former is much more benefical than the latter. The latter will only give you satisfaction and wont push you to do great things. But the former will give you small wins and teach you how to solve bigger problems. Imagine you are a Harvard student obsessed with computers and programming. As a result you would only get an A in the classes which intrest you such as CS 50 or DS&A. Therefore your gen eds such as art history, linguistics or Rome of Agustus would start to fall. You have 2 options either fail the gen eds and puruse programming or attempt to game the system and beat the professor. Failing the gen eds will risk your GPA and make others think you a non intellectual. But gameing the system may teach you valuable skills in how to solve problems such as connecting large groups of people or understaning how to provide value to others. Do you know which one you would choose?
