What is the best invention of the 21st century?

Is it something that saves lives such as cancer research or pencillin. Or rather something that makes us more efficent such as an engine or the wheel. Perhads you argue something like mathematics or a theorem that when applied to other aspects of life it opens doors. Such as Newton with physics or Euler with math.

When you think of the best invention you naturally think of the thing that benefits the most people. Whether it be physcial like a wheel or theoretical like the physics behind one. Its pretty fair to think of the invention its self as important because it is what you use every day. The inventor plants the seed of his invention and grows it into a tree. As a result that tree gives shade, enough for more than the inventor from the beating sun. The size of this tree is different based upon the invention. For example, steve jobs's tree is very big as many people take shade under his invention of apple. But making a new type of app that only benefits a portion of Iphone users means that your tree is smaller. But the thing is that this tree is inside the shade of the bigger tree of apple planted by steve jobs. This idea has sort of an inception factor. Where there could be a product inside of a product. Sort of like a tree-ception. But since there is proof that there can be trees in the shade of bigger trees, how can you go a level above and is there a first tree that inst inside a bigger one. The thing is that every single person sits in the shade of tress planted by others despite the fact if you are planting or not. This means yes to much of the hard work done by Jobs' that he was planting in the shade of some previous invention, some previous technological building blocks that paved part of the way. Therefore stating there is is an infinite amount of trees bigger than the biggest one you can think of. This is sort of like the full hotel but with infinite rooms riddle. Despite the hotel being filled then can take in new people by asking someone to move one room down, and then so on and so on. Therefore the answer to the best invention/ biggest tree is trivial because you cal always argue the fact that they couldnt have created it without previous inventions. Okay so if there is always a bigger tree then how can we judge the best invention. Well one way I propose we do this is by looking at which trees were the hardest to plant relative to their size. For example, trying to plant the idea of eugenics in society will be immensly harder than trying to plant the idea of AI into society. But one will benefit society more and I am not talking about eugenics. Getting society on board with the idea of eugenics is much harder than trying to greate the math behind machine learning or make a product for useres. By just that fact technically eugenics would be a greater invention because it is harder. But its benefit to society is astronomically smaller than the benefit of AI. So then we solved it right? it is just the difficulty of the problem * the benefit. While that is still on the right track I still think it needs to be refined further. One aspect that I thibnk we are missing is the fact that some inventions are going to be made just by human nature. Across Human history people have a found a way to make their work more efficent. Making the benefit per unit spent ratio greater over time. I believe it is a human instinct along with hunger in thirst to start hacking to increase this ratio. Although AI is hard to make and benefical to society I believe it was already going to be made. I dont believe you can stop the human generation from creating it. The reward is just so high that there are monetary forces besides curiosity pushing people to plant the tree. Therefore I think the best invention is something that wasnt going to eventually be made but is hard to make and provide value. My idea is mass information consumption.
